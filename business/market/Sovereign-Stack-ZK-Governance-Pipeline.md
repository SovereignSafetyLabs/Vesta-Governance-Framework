# 1. The Problem: The “Speed vs. Truth” Gap in AI Security

AI is now capable of orchestrating **end-to-end cyber-attacks** with 80–90% automation (Anthropic, 2025).  
This collapses the final constraint protecting trailing-edge enterprises:  
**the human labor bottleneck.**

As offensive AI scales:

- attack costs approach zero  
- attribution becomes difficult or impossible  
- the long tail of legacy systems becomes targetable  
- sophisticated attacks can be executed by novices  
- defensive architecture must shift from manual → automated → verifiable  

The emerging safety dilemma:

### **Fast AI is dangerous because it cannot be verified in time.**  
### **Slow verification (ZK) is safe but cannot run fast enough.**

This is the **Speed vs. Truth Gap**.

| Property | AI Runtime | ZK-Proofs |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| Speed | Instant | Slow |
| Security | Low | Absolute |
| Determinism | Weak | Mathematical |
| Latency | Sub-millisecond | Seconds → minutes |
| Purpose | Act | Verify |

**Conclusion:**  
ZK proofs alone cannot govern real-time inference.  
Real-time inference cannot be trusted without a truth layer.

A **two-tier governance system** is required.
